Research

Research Interests

My research sits at the intersection of blockchain systems, machine learning, and financial economics. I am particularly interested in applying computational and data-driven methods to understand, model, and improve decentralised systems.

Blockchain Forensics & On-Chain Analytics

Applying ML-based anomaly detection on large-scale on-chain transaction data for fraud identification, pump-and-dump pattern recognition, and illicit activity tracing across multi-chain ecosystems.

Token Economy Design & Cryptoeconomics

Studying emergent centralisation phenomena in token distribution, designing incentive-compatible tokenomics, and modelling agent behaviour in DeFi protocols using game theory and network science.

Consensus Mechanism Modelling

Formal analysis and simulation of consensus protocols (hashgraph, PoS, DAG-based) with focus on security guarantees, throughput optimisation, and MEV dynamics.

AI-Powered DeFi & Autonomous Agents

Building and studying LLM-based agentic systems for autonomous on-chain execution, intent-driven transactions, and AI-assisted portfolio management in decentralised markets.
